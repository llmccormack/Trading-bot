# TJR (The Journeyman Trader) — Trading Philosophy

## Core Philosophy
Structure-based trading with patience. The higher timeframe is the boss.
Only take the highest-probability setups that align multiple timeframes.
Quality over quantity — fewer, better trades.

## Core Concept: Multi-Timeframe Confluence
1. **4h or 1h** — determine the dominant trend and key structural levels
2. **15m** — find the setup forming at those levels
3. **5m** — refine entry timing

## Setup Criteria
1. **HTF trend direction** — don't fight the 1h/4h trend
2. **Structural confluence** — entry at a level that is significant on at least 2 timeframes
3. **Market structure break** — a lower timeframe break of structure (BOS) in the trade direction
4. **Order block / fair value gap** (optional) — institutional footprint at the entry zone
5. **Session timing** — prioritize London open and NY open setups

## Entry Rules
- Wait for a 15m candle close confirming the setup
- Enter at market or limit order at the candle midpoint
- Stop: below the structural low being protected (for longs)
- Target: next major HTF resistance level

## Key Concepts
- **Break of Structure (BOS)**: price takes out a prior swing high (bullish) or swing low (bearish)
- **Change of Character (CHOCH)**: first BOS against the prevailing trend — signals potential reversal
- **Order Block**: the last down candle before a big bullish move (potential buy zone)

## Discipline Rules
- If the setup is not clear and clean, skip it — the next one will come
- Max 2-3 trades per session
- If wrong twice in one session, stop trading for the day

## R:R Minimum
- 3:1 minimum — high patience approach requires high reward
